How to improve
📱
Batch your social time
Set two fixed windows (e.g. 12pm and 6pm). This can cut distraction % by 15–20 points.
🕐
Find your peak hours
Use the heatmap to identify your most focused hours and schedule your hardest tasks then.
🎯
Set a daily study goal
Default goal is 240 min. Try to hit this before touching social or entertainment.
🔥
Don't break the chain
Even on bad days, log some study time. A score of 40 keeps your streak alive.
📊
Review your weekly chart
Compare this week vs last week every Sunday. Patterns reveal solutions.
⚡
Aim for score trends
A score of 75 three days in a row beats 95 once and 30 twice. Consistency wins.

How we calculate your scores

Full transparency on the logic behind every number — no black boxes, no mystery. The same inputs always produce the same output.

The formulas
Focus Score Formula
Score =
  (Study × 1.5)
  ÷ (Social × 0.8
     + Ent × 0.6 + Total)
  × 100
Why these weights? Study gets 1.5× because it's the most productive. Social media gets 0.8× penalty (higher — more addictive and disruptive). Entertainment gets 0.6× (lower — some leisure is healthy).
Distraction Percentage
Distraction =
  (Social + Entertainment)
  ÷ Total Screen Time
  × 100
Why 30% threshold? A 70/30 focused-to-leisure split is both sustainable and realistic for students. Above 30% distraction, cognitive switching cost starts to meaningfully reduce study quality.
You can trust your score
Your Focus Score is calculated entirely from data you log. The formula is deterministic — same inputs always produce the same score. No hidden adjustments, no peer comparison, no server uploads. All data stays in your browser. The weights are fixed, transparent, and grounded in productivity research on deep work and cognitive load theory.
